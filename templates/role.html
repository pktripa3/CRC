Role of Medicinal Plants in CRC Cancer


>>According to estimates, colorectal cancer is now the primary cause of cancer-related deaths globally. Numerous evidence-based studies have reported the use of herbal treatments as a substitute therapy, as the majority of existing medications impact important organs including the liver and heart.



>>A wide variety of bioactive components, including those with anti-inflammatory, anti-cancer, antioxidant, and antibacterial actions, are present in medicinal plants. The therapeutic actions of medicinal plants are attributed to the considerable activation of many signalling pathways by phytochemicals found in them. An extensive overview of the medicinal potential of a herbal diet in the treatment of colorectal cancer and other conditions is provided in this paper. The investigation of medicinal plants' relationships with the microbiome and cancer pathways will receive particular focus.



>>An effective hepatoprotective and cardioprotective drug, as well as a treatment option for colorectal cancer, is a medicinal plant high in bioactive chemicals. These bioactive substances have proven to be able to cause apoptosis and stop the proliferation of malignant cells. Medicinal plants that may help prevent cancer and its negative effects include pomegranate, garlic, soybean, olive, green tea, papaya, and grapes. The metabolism of the gut microbiota can be adjusted by bioactive chemicals, and the synthesis of short-chain fatty acids has cardioprotective benefits and lowers the risk of colon cancer. Therefore, it may be concluded that a diet high in medicinal plants and the gut microbiome interact to prevent both cardiac arrest and colorectal cancer.



>>Different strategies are used by plants and their primary chemicals to influence transcription and the cell cycle. PI3K, P-Akt protein, and MMP expression are reduced, superoxide dismutase is induced to eliminate free radicals, DNA oxidation is reduced, apoptosis is induced by inducing a cell cycle arrest in S phase, antiapoptotic Bcl-2 and Bcl-xL proteins are reduced, proliferating cell nuclear antigen (PCNA), cyclin A, cyclin D1, cyclin B1, and cyclin E are among these pathways. Additionally, plant chemicals raise the amounts of BAD, Bax, caspase 3, caspase 7, caspase 8, and caspase 9 proteins as well as cell cycle inhibitors including p53, p21, and p27. Generally speaking, medicinal plants may be able to stop colon cancer cells from growing and proliferating.
